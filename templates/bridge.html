Project Overview

Many children who are delayed in both social and language development are unable to clearly identify or express their emotions, causing tantrums or other more destructive outlets, which in turn results in more frustration on part of both children and caregivers. In order to counteract this problem, we are creating an app for tablets, phones, and laptops that would remove the motor stumbling blocks for the physically challenged users by replacing buttons with the preexisting touchscreen and provide clear audio and visual feedback to relate emotion and language, encouraging constructive expression. In addition, this app will be portable and convenient for both the children and the parents.

My Role

Work Process

Step 1: Identify Problem

People tend to turn to their friends when they are in trouble even if their friends are not nearby. Although mobile phones can let them contact their friends immediately, their problems might not be able to wait until their friends show up. Therefore, in many cases, they have to turn to strangers, but it is not very effective and efficient for them to ask for help one by one. It would be great if there is an application that is designed for people to help strangers.

Step 2: User Research

Because of the time limit, my partner and me could not conduct a complete user research, so we interviewed some of our friends instead. Combining what we learn from our friends and our inspirations from YikYak.com, we discussed the features of out application and created the low-fidelity prototypes.

Step 3: Develop

We spent the most time writing code. It was not easy to use what we had learned to accomplish what we wanted to build, but we decided to challenge ourselves.

Challenge

The biggest challenge that I encountered is to have the latest post shown on the page right after the submission. Share used Google Datastore to store usersâ€™ posts, but it took longer for Datastore to update the newest entity than the page redirection. I made several attempts. For instance, I created a separate page for submission instead of using modal so that the home page would not automatically refresh. We could just update the view first or let the page wait for a little bit time before it refreshes, but we decided not to do that. One night, I was trying to set a time interval so that the posts could be synchronized on multiple screens without refreshing manually. Then I surprisingly realized that the datastore problem could be fixed when I set the time interval to have the ajax function called repeatedly, and I was so happy about it.

Takeaways
