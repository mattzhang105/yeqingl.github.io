Doki·Doki

My Contribution: Wireframe | Prototype | UI Design | Visual Design

CHALLENGE

Our group were given a challenge to design a heart health monitor that can

  1. Measure and track pulse dynamics and other physiological states
  2. Display several key parameters of heart health
  3. Recommend nutrition, stress and exercise options to improve health

OUR SOLUTION

A wearable paired with a mobile app that tracks daily heart health and prevents heart disease due to unhealthy lifestyle in the long run.

INITIAL RESEARCH

Speaking of heart health monitor, the first thing that came into my mind was a device designed specifically for heart disease patients.

However, instead of focusing on a medical solution, we wanted to raise heart health awareness among a larger population.

Our initial research showed that there were three problems that could cause heart disease due to unstable blood pressure.

Fortunately, these problems are totally correctable, which were great entry points for us to solve the problem.

TARGETED USERS

The targeted users are students and working professionals who spend most of their time studying and working and are likely to neglect to live a healthy lifestyle.

USER RESEARCH

We conducted user research to understand lifestyles of our targeted users, their concerns about their health, and current technology they use to track health. Based on our findings, we created a persona.

From the survey, we also learned that wearable could be a good option.

IDEATION

We decided to move forward with a wearable, since compared with the heart health monitor on the market, a wearable is portable, provides real-time information, easy to understand, and affordable.

The idea is a wearable paired with smart phone app. The wearable is used mainly for gathering data from user and the app is used for data interpretation, notifications and detailed information.

I also thought of using gradient outer ring on the wearable to indicate general health condition for long term motivation because lifestyle change is a slow process.

DESIGN & PROTOTYPE

During design, we had a discussion over the initial app structure, since I found there should be stronger connection between the health profile page and the visualization of health condition. Later, a new version with the visualization being the subscreen was created.

We recruited 5 participants for our usability testing. The purpose was to find any problem with the user flow and information display.

Key finding 1: Participants didn’t interpret “doctor” icon as send health profile to a doctor. They thought that was for consulting.

Key finding 2: Participants didn’t realize the recommendations were swipeable and there were three kinds of recommendations in total.
