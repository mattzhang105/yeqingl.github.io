Aqua Music Player


Individual Project | Wireframe | Prototype | UI Design | Interaction Design

This is a 4-week project that I did to illustrate my ideal music player.

BACKGROUND

One day, I was going to play some music on Spotify when relaxing, but I got tired of listening to “my favorites” and wanted to find something new. I clicked on “Browse” and selected “Chill” as the mood. I was shown a lot of relevant playlists and I had no idea which one to choose.

I clicked on a random one and hoped it would match my taste, but it didn’t. So I tried another playlist but still didn’t like it. After a few attempts… “well, maybe I’ll just listen to my favorites.”

Many music apps on the market are undoubtedly powerful. They provide various functions and give people a lot of control. However, to me, instead of giving users a lot of freedom to make decisions, an ideal music app should make decisions for its users because it knows them so well.

Therefore, I decided to design a music player that can make decisions for me and save my efforts.

IDEATION

My design focuses on solving two problems: how to play the right songs and how to adapt to the changes to the user’s taste over time. I figured that the solution should do the following things:

  1. Play the right song
    New users need to complete a quiz about their listening habits. As they listen to more music, the music player will play music that match their tastes.

  2. Play at an appropriate frequency
    Users are unlikely to have equal preference to each song in their “favorites”, so the the music player records users’ behaviors when they listen to their favorites and figures out an appropriate frequency for each song.

  3. Visualize listening history
    It’s an interesting add-on feature to help users describe their music tastes.

SKETCHES

I did brainstorm about how the tree should look like. The idea of the leaves were not chosen because as the tree grew luxuriant, it would be hard to tell which branch the leaves belong to.

WIREFRAMES & PROTOTYPE

The wireframes were created based on the main user flow for testing purpose. Genre was chosen to be visualized because it is more descriptive and accurate than other categories, such as mood or occasions.



Since I only had time to do one serie of usability testing, I decided to add colors and make them higher fidelity so that I could get more feedbacks.

Based on the feedbacks, I changed the color theme from blue to green to make the music player more natural and friendly. I also replaced the hamburger menu with a navigation bar to make the user flow clear.

FINAL DESIGN

Based on the feedbacks, I changed the color theme from blue to green to make the music player more natural and friendly. I also replaced the hamburger menu with a navigation bar to make the user flow clear.

The final design accomplishes the three things mentioned above.

FUTURE

The quiz and the app feature should be more matched. One of the questions asks “In what occasions do you listen to music?” and the options include “driving”, “running”, “relaxing”, etc. The idea was to help the music player to provide the right songs in different occasions, but the app doesn’t show an intention to learn about when it’s used. Therefore, I’m interested in exploring more about this in the future to make the app smarter.
