Aqua Music Player

Aqua is your unique music player that remembers, learns, and visualizes user's listening habit.

June 2015 - July 2015, Individual Project

ideation

Everyone has their own music player. At that time, I was using a Chinese music player called Douban FM. Instead of giving users a wide range of control, its simplicity allows very limited interactions and wants users to “meet their music by chance” as its vision statement. Users can’t search for a specific song / singer or customize a playlist (the active search function is enabled in its latest version, which makes me a little sad because I think sometimes it’s good to be passive and enjoy the given beauty).

Using a music player is like building a unique friendship. The longer time you use it, the better it knows about you. You don’t have to tell it about what you like, and it already knows. Until that summer, I had been using Douban FM everyday for almost a year. During that period of time, I can feel the change of my taste and the music that Douban FM plays changes along with me. It is a unique existence to me and everyone’s Douban FM is unique. Therefore, I decided to design a music player that enhances the uniqueness.

design

Iteration 1

I came up with an idea that the music player visualizes user’s listening history and taste. I first thought of a planet whose surface grows a little tree that will form a unique shape and inside is composed of the top 4 genres the user listens to.


Iteration 2

Then I realized that it is unnecessary to show the user percentage since the numbers don’t mean a lot to them and without the numbers it’s not obvious to see the difference between each genre. Therefore, I decided to remove the planet and combine its function to the tree.


Iteration 3

This iteration focuses on the UI. I changed the theme color from blue to green because some of my feedback said that the blue tree created a supernatural feeling and not very friendly. I also changed the hamburger menu to a tab bar since it’s easier to switch between different views and easier to see which screen the user is interacting with.
