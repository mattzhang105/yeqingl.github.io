Seattleites' Travel Trend


My Contribution: Problem Defining | Front-End Development

PROBLEM

Most travel planning tools on the Internet required either a specific destination or a travel time. For people who do not have a clear preference for when and where to go, determining where to go is difficult due to a lack of inspiration.

Therefore, by visualizing the monthly popular destinations from Seattle over the year 2016, we aimed to provide inspirations of places to visit for people traveling from Seattle.

DESIGN

We used a visualization methodology called Five Design Sheets to guide the ideation process and finalize the design.

Sheet 1: Brainstorm

At this stage, we each came up with a few ideas to visualize travel trend.

Sheet 2 / 3 / 4: Initial Designs

Then we generalized three different designs and did analysis to them.

Sheet 5: Realization Design

After our discussion, the map visualization was selected. Instead of realizing the design at this stage, we created a paper prototype for testing.

TEST & IMPLEMENT

After testing the paper prototype, we made changes to our design and implemented into interactive visualization using D3.js.

Key finding 1: The context and purpose were not clear enough. Participants couldn’t interpret the visualization as a map immediately without outline. Since the visualization didn’t have a title, they didn’t know what it was about until they finished the tasks.

Key finding 2: Visualization was crowded with information. Since all destinations had their names labeled and were connected with Seattle, the image looked a little crowded. Also, the circles were overlapping each other, which made it difficulty to interact with.

FINAL VISUALIZATION

We made some changes according to the findings.

Add titles: We added titles to both pages of the visualization.

Mitigate occlusion: We removed lines and labels on the circles and allowed the map to zoom in and out to decrease occlusion.
